\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We evaluate \modelname{} on three datasets: Assembly, H2O, and BigHands. Following the current evaluation logs, we report Average Displacement Error (ADE), Final Displacement Error (FDE), Average Pairwise Distance (APD), CMD, and FID for all compared models. Lower ADE, FDE, CMD, and FID indicate better forecasting accuracy and distribution matching, while higher APD indicates greater sample diversity.

Table~\ref{tab:assembly_by_action} reports Assembly results grouped by action, arranged as pairs of subtables across three rows. Table~\ref{tab:cross_dataset} places the H2O and BigHands results side by side. We keep explicit \texttt{TODO} markers for the Assembly actions whose \modelname{} runs are still missing from the current results file.

Overall, the Assembly results show that \modelname{} is consistently strong on the action subsets for which all required runs are already available. It achieves the best ADE on \textit{attempt}, \textit{rotate}, and \textit{unscrew}, and it also obtains the best FDE on \textit{attempt} and the best FID on \textit{attempt}, \textit{rotate}, and \textit{unscrew}. The \textit{remove} subset is more competitive, where comusion slightly improves ADE, FDE, CMD, and FID, but \modelname{} remains the second-best ADE model and retains low distributional error. On H2O, \modelname{} remains competitive but is not the best model under ADE and FDE: comusion attains the strongest reconstruction accuracy, while \modelname{} is second-best in ADE and maintains a substantially lower FID than several older baselines. On BigHands, \modelname{} becomes one of the strongest models overall, ranking second on ADE, tying for best on CMD and FID, and remaining competitive on FDE, while the highest APD values are obtained by other methods.

Figure~\ref{fig:tradeoff_apd_ade} complements the tables by visualizing the diversity-quality tradeoff through APD and ADE. The main pattern is that the methods with the largest diversity are not necessarily the strongest overall generators: for instance, gsps tends to push farther toward high APD, but this often comes with weaker forecast quality. In contrast, \modelname{} consistently remains in a favorable low-error region, especially on Assembly and BigHands, without relying on extreme APD. This suggests that the proposed model preserves multimodality while keeping the samples close to the target motion distribution.

These trends are consistent with the theoretical formulation introduced in Sec.~\ref{sec:method}. Since \modelname{} performs diffusion in a residual space aligned with articulated hand topology and frequency-dependent motion modes, its stochastic refinement is not imposed in an unconstrained isotropic latent space. Empirically, this structure appears to improve sample consistency with the target distribution: across several Assembly actions and on BigHands, \modelname{} combines strong ADE/FDE with particularly competitive FID and CMD values. We therefore interpret the results as evidence that the spatio-spectral formulation improves not only forecasting accuracy, but also the quality-consistency tradeoff of the generated futures.
